Contracting for 3D Buildng Models

3D City Modeling Request for Capabilities and Cost Estimates

The BPDA has been maintaining a collection of 3D building and bridge models from various sources. Our model management tools and schema makes it possible manage a collection of diverse 3d buildng models, incorporating updates and models of development proposals while preserving historical information.

Existng Model Collection to be Updated

  • Wholesale LOD 2 models form a city-wide 2011 photogrammetric survey
  • Articulted LOD 3 models for specific planning areas created using the same 2011 survey
  • Hand-made models representing large projects that have been proposed. Many of these are now standing in for actual buildings that have been built.
  • Hand-made models of bridges.
  • Photogrammetrically-captured models of demolished buildings are preserved as a historical record.

Ideal Specification for Updates

We are now hoping to update the model collection with 3D models of buildngs that have been built since 2011.

  • We are not planning to do a special fly-over for this project. We are hoping to provide already-licensed stereo imagery with control and or LIDAR as needed. If a 3D model provider had access to recent source data covering Boston, Massachusetts, this would be great.
  • We do not need to generate 3d models of every building in the city, but would consider this, if the quality of the new models is substantially improved over our existing models.
  • We would be able to outline specific new buildings that need to be modeled, but if a vendor was able to discover changes that would be interesting.
  • We hope to have models with true 3D roof details, and a decent representation of the sides of buildings.
  • We require efficient low-polygon models. Preferably the model wil be jeneralized so that flat planes are represented with a minimum of triangles and with an angular tolerance that maintains right-angles and parallel features.
  • Textures are not necessary, but would be interesting to see what the extra charge would be for this.
  • We recognize that it can be difficult to capture details near ground level. Ideally, the new models could be created with a below-ground margin to avoid floating corners that appear when different terrain models are used.
  • Extra consideration is given to modelers who are able to provide closed/water-tight models that perform as 3d solids in boolean operations.
  • We are interested in models that are free of use-restrictions.
  • We prefer models to be deliverd in OBJ format, but may be able to use collada or multipatch.
  • It would be useful to have an estimate that includes a set-up fee and a charge per building or square kilometer.
